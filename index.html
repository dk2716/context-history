Why Context exists

You keep hearing the same names and events — and never quite getting the full story.

When a news story mentions Kissinger, Pinochet, or the Responsibility to Protect, it assumes you already know the background. Context gives you that background — not as a dry encyclopedia entry, but as a real explanation of what happened, why it mattered, and what it still means today.

Every article pulls from multiple academic sources, names the contested evidence, and connects historical events to the world you're actually living in. Think of it as the conversation a great history teacher would have with you — if you had one on hand at 11pm.

Multi-source

Every claim is attributed. Every contested figure is flagged. We cite the historians, not just the Wikipedia summary of what they said.

Connected to today

Each article ends with a "Why It Matters Today" section — not because history repeats itself, but because the same decisions, dynamics, and power structures keep resurfacing.

Honest about complexity

We don't flatten contested history into simple heroes and villains. The Legacy & Debate section in every article reflects what serious scholars actually argue about — not just what's comfortable.
